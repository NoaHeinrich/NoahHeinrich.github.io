Noah Heinrich

I am a web developer, who enjoys using his skills and knowledge in service of a greater purpose. Whether I am writing code, prose, or lesson plans, I take immense satisfaction in overcoming obstacles in order to assemble a high quality product. I am accustomed to changing conditions, and feel most alive outside of my comfort zone.

In October, 2015, I enrolled in Dev Bootcamp, because I felt that my life and career needed a new direction. Until then, my only experience in programming had been one semester of C++. Now, I am proficient with Ruby, JavaScript, HTML5 and CSS3. Additionally, I have become a more empathic team member, and have grown as both a leader and a full-stack developer.

My purpose in life has always been helping others, and I hope to do so in my new career. As a corps member, and then a team leader with City Year Detroit, I was able to exercise my communication skills in service to furthering the education of at-risk youth. It was during this time that I first noticed how under-utilized technology is in the classroom. I knew then that one key to closing the country's education gap is making sure that web and mobile technology is not limited to schools in wealthy districts.

Whether in Detroit or Chicago, I take pride in my community, and the challenges it overcomes every day. Being able to pursue my newfound craft while raising my city up would make me happier than I can easily put into words.
